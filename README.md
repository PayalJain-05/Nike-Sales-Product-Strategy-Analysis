# Nike-Sales-Product-Strategy-Analysis
Analyzed 100 customer reviews across Reddit, official site, and marketplaces. Complete Tableau dashboard surfacing consumer pain points and strategy insights.
Nike Sales & Product Strategy Analysis

Overview

I analyzed 100 customer reviews for Nike, pulled from Reddit, the official website, and online marketplaces, to understand what customers actually think about the products they're buying. The goal wasn't just to report ratings, but to figure out why certain products score low, where the biggest complaints cluster, and what Nike could realistically fix to improve satisfaction and retention.

Why I Did This

Customer reviews are messy. They mix numbers (star ratings) with opinions (free text), and most of the useful signal is buried in the text. I wanted to turn that unstructured feedback into something a product or business team could actually act on, not just "customers are happy" or "customers are unhappy," but which products, which platforms, and which specific issues.

My Approach

1. Getting the data ready
Before I could analyze anything, I had to structure the reviews. For each of the 100 reviews I:


Classified the sentiment as positive, negative, or neutral
Tagged each review with a keyword representing its main topic — comfort, durability, price, design, performance, or general


This step mattered the most. Without tagging why someone left a review, all I'd have is a rating number with no explanation behind it.

2. Digging into the numbers
Once the data was tagged, I built pivot tables in Excel to break things down by:


Total review counts
Average ratings per feature (comfort, durability, price, etc.)
Performance by platform (Reddit, website, marketplace)
Performance by product category (shoes, apparel, accessories)


3. Visualizing it
I then built a Tableau dashboard so the findings weren't just sitting in a spreadsheet, but could be explored visually: satisfaction drivers, problem areas, category performance, and recurring customer concerns, all in one view.

What I Found

The biggest surprise was that Nike's flagship category, shoes, is actually its weakest performer:


Shoes averaged 3.22, the lowest of any category, and dropped even further to 2.73 on the website specifically
Apparel averaged 4.23, the strongest category, and performed especially well on the marketplace
Accessories averaged 4.14, and performed best on the website


Digging into the keyword tags, two problems came up again and again:


Durability — customers weren't happy with how long products lasted, especially shoes
Price — a recurring concern across categories, suggesting a gap between price and perceived value


On the flip side, customers consistently praised comfort, design, and performance, so the brand isn't struggling everywhere. It's a fairly specific problem centered on shoe quality and pricing.

What This Means for the Business

Based on what the data showed, I'd recommend:


Prioritize durability improvements in shoes — this is the single clearest, most repeated complaint
Revisit pricing strategy — price came up too often as a pain point to ignore
Tighten quality control, particularly for the shoe line, before it further drags down brand perception
Lean into what's already working — comfort and design are genuine strengths worth highlighting in marketing


A Note on the Data

This was a self-directed analysis project for learning purposes. The dataset is sample-based, partly AI-assisted in construction, and modeled on realistic review patterns, it doesn't represent actual Nike data or internal company information.
